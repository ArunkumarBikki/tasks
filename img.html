ROYAL CHALLENGERS BENGALURU

Retained Players:

  • Virat Kohli

    Virat Kohli was retained by the Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) for the 2025 season for a salary of ₹21 crore, making him the team's top retention. Kohli's retention was expected, given his long history with the team, which dates back to the IPL's inception in 2008:

    Kohli became the second-costliest retention ahead of IPL 2025 mega auction, with RCB paying him a staggering ₹21 crore to continue being part of the next three-year-cycle. Kohli has been the only player in the history of the IPL to have continued with the same team throughout.

  • Rajat Patidar

    Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) retained Rajat Patidar for the 2025 Indian Premier League (IPL) for INR 11 crore. Patidar is a right-handed batter from Madhya Pradesh who has played three Tests and one ODI for India. He has been a key part of RCB's batting unit and has scored 799 runs in 27 IPL matches.

    Patidar expressed his gratitude to RCB for retaining him and for the faith they have shown in him. He also thanked the fans for their support and hoped it would continue in the upcoming season.

  • Yash Dayal

    Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) retained Yash Dayal for the IPL 2025 season for ₹5 crore. Dayal was one of three players retained by RCB, along with Virat Kohli and Rajat Patidar.

    Dayal expressed gratitude for RCB's faith in him, especially during difficult times. He said, "RCB believed in me even in my lowest moments. That is very special for me".

    Andy Flower, RCB's bowling coach, explained the rationale behind retaining Dayal, saying that his unique left-arm bowling style adds value to the team's bowling attack.

Un-Retained Players:

  • Mohammed Siraj

    Siraj has been an integral part of RCB’s pace battery since linking up with the franchise at the 2018 IPL Auction for a sum of INR 2.2 Crore, and since then has picked up 83 wickets from 87 matches for the team.

    Now an India international across formats, Siraj’s non-retention did come as a surprise to quite a few fans, but in an episode of RCB Bold Diaries, head coach Andy Flower and Director of Cricket Mo Bobat added weight to the topic and insights into why the right-arm seamer was not retained.

    Flower said, “I'm sure one of the surprises around our retentions will be the fact that we haven't retained Mohamed Siraj. Mohamed Siraj is a successful international cricketer of some standing and has also been with RCB for the last eight years. We want to look for some particular skills in the fast bowling department. We've already identified and retained one of those particular skills that we want, which is the left arm angle and T20 skills that Yash Dayal brings us.”

  • Glenn Maxwell

    Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) and Australia all-rounder Glenn Maxwell opened up on not being retained by his team ahead of the 2025 mega auction. Notably, RCB retained three players for the upcoming season namely Virat Kohli (INR 21 Cr), Rajat Patidar (INR 11 Cr) and Yash Dayal (INR 5 Crore) using INR 37 Crore.

    The Bengaluru-based franchise has three Right to Match (RTM) cards available with them along with the remaining auction purse amount of INR 83 Crore. Among the big names not retained by the franchise was Glenn Maxwell , who’s been a part of the team since 2021. Recently, the all rounder opened up on how the franchise conveyed the message to him and revealed that he was impressed by their handling of the situation.

    “I got a phone call from Mo Bobat and Andy Flower. I sat there on a zoom call, and they sort of explained to me the decision not to be retained. And, it was actually a really beautiful exit meeting, if that if that could be possible. We ended up talking about the game for about half an hour and talking about their strategy and what they were looking to get to do, going forward. So, I was really happy with that,” said Maxwell on ESPNCricinfo’s Around the wicket show.

  • Faf du Plesis

    Former South Africa captain Faf du Plessis bid adieu to the Royal Challengers Bengaluru with an emotional message on Instagram. In a heartfelt note, Du Plessis bid farewell to the franchise and expressed his gratitude to his RCB teammates, loyals fans and the team management. The 40-year-old said that he would cherish his time spent at RCB. The franchise did not retain their former captain Du Plessis ahead of the auction. Even during the IPL 2025 mega auction, RCB did not exercise their Right to Match option for Du Plessis.

    "As my chapter close with RCB I want to take a moment to reflect on what an incredible journey this has been. When I joined three years ago, I had no idea how this journey would unfold. But I fell in love with the city of Bengaluru and the amazing people of RCB. This place and its people have become a part of who I am, and I'll carry these memories and the connections I've buit with me forever. Thank you for making these three years so special," Du Plessis wrote on Instagram.

    "Playing at the Chinnaswamy Stadium has been one of the most electric experiences of my career. The energy, the passion, and the support from the fans make it truly one of a kind. Every time I stepped onto that field, the atmosphere was nothing short of magical."
